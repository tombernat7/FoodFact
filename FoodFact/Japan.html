For the next two countries (Japan and China), the selections of the 3 dishes will be totally subjective. As I am not a big fan of Asian cuisine, I will choose the few dishes dishes that appeal to me from each culture.

1. Sushis

Obviously when I'm thinking about japanese food, I am directly thinking about sushis. Salmon, rice, sometimes avocado or an algae leaf: this food combo is unique. And a unique combo means an incredible recipe for a unique moment.

2. Gyoza

Gyoza are savoury moon-shaped dumplings, made from a minced mixture of savoury fillings (pork mince, cabbage, green onion and mushroom is a common combination, but other fillings can be used as well) which are wrapped up in a circular gyoza wrapper and crimped or pleated around the edges to make an iconic half-moon shape. Gyoza dumplings are normally cooked by frying on one side (a process that gives the gyoza a crisp, savoury bottom), and then steaming for 2-3 minutes so that the rest of the wrapper is smooth and silky, and the filling inside is moist and juicy. 3. Ramen

Ramen is a noodle soup dish consisting of wheat noodles (also known as 'ramen noodles'), a savoury broth (soy sauce, salt, miso, and tonkotsu pork bone are the four main ramen broth bases) and toppings of meat, protein, spring onions, bamboo shoots, and others. Ramen is one of present-day Japan’s absolute favourite delicacies, costing very little and being widely available in restaurants and ramen bars (which are on almost every street corner).
